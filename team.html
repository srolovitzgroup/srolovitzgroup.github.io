Department Profile

David Srolovitz specializes materials theory, modeling, and simulations, with a particular emphasis on defects in crystals, interfaces such as grain boundaries, microstructure evolution, and the optoelectronic properties of materials. He is a Member of the US National Academy of Engineering and a Fellow of several organizations, including the Materials Research Society (MRS), The Minerals, Metals & Materials Society (TMS), ASM International, and the Institute of Physics.

Dr. Srolovitz has been recognized for his significant contributions to the field with the MRS Materials Theory Award. Throughout his career, he has held various prominent positions, including staff roles at Exxon Corporate Research and the Los Alamos National Laboratory, and served as the Executive Director of the Institute for High Performance Computing in Singapore. His academic tenure includes appointments at Princeton University, the University of Pennsylvania, the University of Michigan, Yeshiva University, and the City University of Hong Kong.
